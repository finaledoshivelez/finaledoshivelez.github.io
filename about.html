I've always enjoyed how words and stories can take us to new places and change how we see old ones. When I was two, I refused to let my parents return an illustrated version of the Wizard of Oz to the library for six months. By the time I finished elementary school, I'd read our Merriam-Webster dictionary cover-to-cover over ten times--and every book in the children's section of our library.

In college, I minored in creative writing. I also had the pleasure of being part of the Cambridge Writers of Imaginative Literature during my masters in the UK, during which time I published a few short works.

These days, I'm a professor in Computer Science at Harvard University. I develop new techniques in artificial intelligence, with applications to healthcare and technology policy. I love how an important part of my job involves communicating with undergraduate and graduate students in computer science, policy makers and legal scholars, clinicians and hospital administrators, business executives, even primary school students. If you're curious about the left brain of my life, check out my Harvard website and my group's website.
